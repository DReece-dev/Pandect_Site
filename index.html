Community guidelines

Pandect is built on a set of guidelines to ensure a consistent quality of
content, an equal playing field for each member of the community and a
democratic approach to journalism. Our guidelines are constantly evolving as
our community grows.

Source

All sources must have a reputation for upholding facts and
need to have an editorial oversight. Furthermoer, sources
that reply on extremist views, rumours or gossip, or only
personal optinions are not valid.

Langyage & Tone

Pantributors are urged to uphold the community's
standards, including neutrality of language and tone,
originality of wording and factuality of content.

Opinions

News card should never show the Pantributor's opinion.
Opinions of public figures are allowed as long as quoted
and marked as such.

Review Process

To avoid false, untruthful or fragmentary information, each
card is reviewed by at least two other Pantributors before
being published. Therefore ensuring the community's standard of quality.

Categories & Tags

Each card has to be filed under one single category. Tags
for people, locations and organisations are added
automatically, thought Pantributors are given the option to
add or change them if necessary.

Topics

A topic is either generated when there are more than three
cards for a particular event, or added by a Pantributor
when seen as relevant. Topics consist of card that are
linked to one specific event.
